How can technology connect humans better, online and in person?

In the early days of the internet, I connected with people on the text-only internet relay chat (IRC) for the first time, writing whatever I thought. To speak without a filter to a wide, anonymous audience had not been possible til then. It was liberating and addictive.

As IRC evolved to graphical interface Netscape Navigator, and robust, global social networks like Facebook, this way of connecting brings us closer -- and also drives us apart.
